# Meta-Review — "Un encodeur auto-supervisé factorisé pour graphes de programmes (IR)" (JEPA-ASM / jepa-v2)

## 1. Summary and panel consensus

The submission is an encoder-only technical note that trains a from-scratch per-relation GNN over ProGraML LLVM-IR program graphs and factors the embedding into `z_sem` (trained invariant to the `-O` optimization level) and `z_speed` (trained invariant to program identity), using a class-conditional VICReg loss (invariance + variance + covariance) plus a Barlow-Twins-style cross-covariance decorrelation term — with, by the authors' own statement, "pas de masquage, pas de prédicteur, pas de cible EMA." Its two honest empirical findings are a pre-training "gate" showing the learnable optimization signal on isolated ExeBench functions is ~1 bit (O2≈O3 for 98.4% of functions), and a reproducible VICReg normalization bug (sum-vs-mean over dimensions hid a factor `d`, `α_eff≈2400 ≫ β=25`) that collapsed `z_sem` to ~3 effective dimensions until fixed (effective rank 2.97→72.4 at constant data). All four reviewers — spanning SSL/collapse theory, compiler-ML, empirical rigor, and novelty/positioning — return **reject at confidence 4**, and the panel is unanimous on the drivers: zero downstream evidence, no baselines, a tautological headline metric, a mis-applied "JEPA" label, and no methodological novelty. The panel is equally unanimous that the note is unusually candid — Section 6 pre-empts nearly every one of these objections itself. The consensus is best stated as: *the paper diagnoses its own fatal flaws honestly but does not resolve any of them, and acknowledgment is not evidence.*

## 2. Decision

**Reject** (resubmittable as a refocused diagnostics note). Justification: judged by proof rather than ambition, every headline claim is currently either tautological (`cos_intra(z_sem)=0.995` over byte-identical views), unproven (no downstream probe, no baseline, single run), or mislabeled ("JEPA", "semantic", "speed", "sans aucun label") — the artifact's genuine contributions are real but too narrow to clear a top venue in their present form.

Note for the record: the adjudication panel correctly finds each individual "fatal" to be *fixable* with bounded work, which would ordinarily argue for major revision. I nonetheless land on reject because the fixes are not one experiment but a coordinated re-scoping — new baselines, a downstream probe, distinct-graph metrics, a rename, and a related-work section — that together change the paper's story rather than patch it. That is a resubmission, not a revision.

## 3. Weaknesses that most determine the verdict (deduplicated)

**W1 — No downstream evidence and no baselines of any kind. The central claim is currently unfalsifiable.** Every Section 4.2 number (cosine, silhouette, effective rank) is an absolute quantity reported against nothing: no random-init encoder, no plain (non-factored) VICReg, no Barlow-Twins/SimCLR/BYOL on the same graphs, no supervised upper bound, and no transfer task. As Reviewer 2 (empirical rigor) notes, a random-initialized GNN on byte-identical inputs would also yield `cos_intra≈1.0`, so the flagship metric cannot distinguish learning from a deterministic map, and `erank=72/96` is uninterpretable because random features are also high-rank. This is the one weakness all four reviewers rank first. **Fixable, not conceptually fatal.** Minimal resolution: (a) a random-init control on the exact 4.2 metrics; (b) frozen linear probes — `-O` from `z_speed`, program/opcode-bucket from `z_sem` — each reported against the random-init control and a bag-of-opcodes/node-count baseline; (c) at least one compiler-relevant readout (nearest-neighbour `z_sem` retrieval of an O0 graph's optimized sibling, or a ProGraML/DevMap-style probe). All are hours-to-days on the existing `checkpoints/encoder.pt`.

**W2 — The headline invariance metric is tautological and never reported on graph-distinct pairs.** The authors' own gate shows O1=O2=O3 graphs are byte-identical for ~75–98% of programs, so 3 of 4 "views" are literally the same input; `cos_intra(z_sem)=0.995` therefore partly measures input identity, not learned invariance. The genuinely learned quantity — O0↔optimized invariance — is never isolated, despite the authors requesting exactly this in Section 6.1. **Fixable, and the severity is arguably overstated** (O0 is always distinct, so it enters half of the pairwise cosines; the number is offset-inflated rather than fully vacuous). Minimal resolution: recompute `cos_intra(z_sem)` on graph-distinct (O0-vs-optimized) pairs only, report it as the headline, and add the random-init floor. This is minutes of compute on cached embeddings and converts a confound into a controlled result.

**W3 — Pervasive mislabeling: "JEPA", "sans aucun label", "semantic", "speed", "disentanglement".** Per the KG's JEPA god-node (41 edges), JEPA *is* latent-space prediction — a context encoder plus a predictor of a masked/target latent, typically with an EMA target (BYOL → I-JEPA → V-JEPA). This model has none of these; it belongs to the "Heritage: EBM, Siamese & Collapse Theory" family (VICReg/Barlow-Twins/SimSiam). Compounding errors: "sans aucun label" is contradicted because `prog(i)` and `spd(i)=π(ℓ_i)` are supervised groupings that define the positives; pushing all four views of one program to cosine 0.995 while `cos_inter=0.10` is instance discrimination over program identity (cf. InstDisc, Wu et al. 2018) — encoding *which* program, the opposite of "ce que fait le code"; and "speed" names a ~1-bit O0-vs-optimized graph-change proxy with no runtime, latency, or throughput ever measured. **Fixable, and largely free** — it is writing, not experiments. Minimal resolution: rename to something like *Factored/Conditional-VICReg on IR graphs*, replace "semantic/speed" with "program-invariant / -O-invariant subspace," and replace "disentanglement without labels" with "representation invariant to two known supervised groupings." Self-flagging the misnomer in Section 6.3 does not license keeping the label on the cover.

**W4 — No novel method, no related work, and an independent rediscovery of a solved failure mode.** The objective is VICReg (with the canonical `(α,β,γ)=(25,25,1)` of Bardes–Ponce–LeCun 2022) plus the off-diagonal Barlow-Twins term (Zbontar et al. 2021) applied across two heads; the backbone is R-GCN / the ProGraML message-passing scheme. The note contains no reference list and mis-names its own substrate "ProgramML" (correctly ProGraML, Cummins et al., arXiv 2003.10536). Critically, the Section 4.3 bug is a textbook instance of the hand-tuned-coefficient fragility that **LeJEPA + SIGReg** (Balestriero & LeCun, arXiv 2511.08544) were explicitly built to eliminate via a dimension-invariant, provable push toward an isotropic-Gaussian embedding — and the fix is incomplete: after sum→mean, the covariance term `C` (normalized by `d` over ~`d²` entries) and cross term `X` (normalized by `max(d_sem,d_speed)=96` over 3072 entries) still carry hidden ~`d` factors, so `(25,25,1)` act on non-comparably-normalized terms. **Fixable as a reframing**, but only by lowering the claim: position this as an *application + diagnostics* note, cite VICReg / Barlow-Twins / R-GCN / ProGraML, and engage the directly overlapping prior art it currently ignores — **C-JEPA** (arXiv 2410.19560, the nearest VICReg+JEPA work), **Graph-JEPA** (arXiv 2309.16014), **LLM-JEPA** (arXiv 2509.14252), and **LeJEPA/SIGReg** as the principled remedy to the very fragility the bug exposes.

**W5 — The `z_speed` half is degenerate, the cross-decorrelation mechanism is unablated, and there is no statistical rigor.** `z_speed` solves a ~1-bit problem with a 32-dim head (effective rank 2.64/32); the off-target silhouette of −0.907 is largely an artifact of that near-1-D collapse, so "each block ignores the other factor" is nearly automatic. The term `X` is literally named "le terme de désentrelacement," yet is never removed (`λ_x=0`) to show it is necessary — and under the crossed program×level sampler it is ≈0 once invariance holds, so it may contribute no real gradient; moreover zero cross-covariance is second-order decorrelation, not independence. Everything is a single run: no seeds, no CIs, so even the flagship 2.97→72.4 result is n=1. **Fixable.** Minimal resolution: a `λ_x=0` ablation plus an `(α,β,γ)`/`λ_x` sensitivity sweep (which the paper's own thesis demands), ≥5 seeds with mean±std on the collapse-bug numbers, merging {O2,O3} or shrinking `d_speed`, and a nonlinear independence check (cross-block MI or a probe predicting one block from the other).

## 4. If the authors do only three things

1. **Rename and reframe honestly.** Drop "JEPA" (and any "assembly"/"world model" framing — the inputs are LLVM IR, and there is no predictor, prediction, dynamics, or EMA target); call it *Factored/Conditional-VICReg on IR graphs*, replace "semantic/speed/disentanglement without labels" with "invariance to two supervised groupings," add a real reference section, and correct "ProgramML" → ProGraML. This alone removes three of the panel's five objections at zero experimental cost.

2. **Add the two baselines that make every metric interpretable, on graph-distinct pairs.** A random-init encoder control and a bag-of-opcodes/O0-detector control, with `cos_intra(z_sem)` recomputed only on O0↔optimized (distinct-graph) pairs, plus a frozen linear probe (`-O` from `z_speed`, program/opcode-bucket from `z_sem`). This converts the "unfalsifiable / tautological" verdict into a controlled, possibly-favorable result — and if the probes only match the graph baseline, that is still an honest, publishable confirmation of your own 1-bit gate.

3. **Compare against the methods the panel names, and ablate the mechanism you claim.** Position against LeJEPA/SIGReg (the coefficient-free, dimension-invariant fix to the exact fragility you rediscovered) and C-JEPA/Graph-JEPA (actual predictor+EMA JEPA, if the label is to be defended at all); and run the `λ_x=0` ablation with ≥5 seeds so the "cross-covariance produces disentanglement" claim is actually tested rather than asserted.

## 5. What is genuinely novel and worth keeping

Be fair: this note has real, citable value that a reject should not erase. First, the **capacity "gate"** (Section 4.1) is a genuinely useful, honest negative result — measuring, *before* training, that isolated-function IR contains only ~1 bit of learnable optimization signal (O0≠O1 100%, O2≈O3 98.4%) bounds what any speed/phase-ordering downstream task could achieve and pre-empts a whole class of overclaiming that most SSL papers skip. Second, the **VICReg normalization-bug case study** (Section 4.3) is a clean, reproducible, pedagogically valuable cautionary tale: isolating that summing-not-averaging over `d` silently sets `α_eff≈25d≈2400 ≫ β=25` and crushes the only rank-creating terms — verified by the fact that adding data (3000→8000) moved rank nothing while the sum→mean fix moved it 2.97→72.4 — is a concrete, community-relevant data point that grounds *why* dimension-invariant objectives like SIGReg matter, and it stands independent of any downstream task. Third, the **engineering hygiene** is above bar for a note: released checkpoint + vocab, a deterministic anti-leak hash split, a principled effective-rank metric `erank(Z)=exp(H(σ/‖σ‖₁))`, and 24 unit tests including normalization guardrails. Finally, and most rare, the **intellectual honesty** of Section 6 is a genuine asset — the paper names its own tautology, its 1-bit ceiling, its label problem, and its missing downstream test. The correct outcome is not to punish that candor but to ask the authors to convert their own open questions (6.1, 6.4, 6.5, 6.6) into the experiments and reframing above and resubmit as what this actually is: an honest VICReg-family diagnostics note on IR-graph representations.
